Understanding Where You Are

Since your lessons have been running 6-7 weeks already, they're likely working on Phase II material in both two-step and waltz. Round dancing is organized into phases (I-VI), with Phase I being basics and Phase II adding more variety and turns.

Best Free Resources

1. Round Dance Instruction (RDInstruction.co.uk) - PRIMARY RESOURCE

This is an excellent FREE site created by UK instructor Judi Read, specifically designed for refreshing skills:

  • Two-Step Online Course
  • Waltz Online Course

Each course includes:

  • Written "talk & walk through" definitions
  • Pictures showing foot positions
  • Video clips demonstrating figures
  • Practice modules with music
  • Organized by progression (Part 1, Part 2, etc.)

2. RoundDancing.net

Harold & Meredith Sears' comprehensive reference:

  • Two-Step Figures
  • Waltz Figures

Great for looking up specific figures and understanding their descriptions.

3. YouTube Channels

  • Search for "round dance two-step tutorial" or "round dance waltz tutorial"
  • Look for channels that post Roundalab-standard instruction
  • The Shoeleather Express has organized playlists by rhythm

Your 2-3 Week Practice Plan

Week 1: Fundamentals Review

Two-Step Focus:

  • Basic rhythm (quick-quick-slow)
  • Forward two-step
  • Box and reverse box
  • Two turning two-steps

Waltz Focus:

  • 3/4 timing (1-2-3, 1-2-3)
  • Box step
  • Balance steps
  • Progressive basics

Practice: 20-30 minutes, 3-4 times this week. Use RDInstruction.co.uk courses, work through Part 1 of each rhythm.

Week 2: Building Complexity

Two-Step:

Add scissors, progressive scissors, hitches, forward locks, fishtail

Waltz:

Add turning box, progressive waltz, underarm turns, hesitations

Practice: 30-40 minutes, 4-5 times. Move to Part 2 on RDInstruction.co.uk. Start practicing to music using your playlists.

Week 3: Integration & Polish

  • Practice complete sequences
  • Focus on smooth transitions
  • Work on timing with music
  • Review any figures that feel rusty

Key Things to Remember

Round Dance Specifics:

  • Cuer calls ahead: The cuer announces figures just before you do them
  • Line of Dance (LOD): Move counter-clockwise around the floor
  • Phase system: Don't worry about memorizing entire dances - that's the cuer's job!
  • Positions matter: Closed, semi-closed, open, butterfly, banjo, sidecar

Two-Step Essentials:

  • Rhythm: Quick-Quick-Slow (QQS)
  • No rise and fall (unlike waltz)
  • Small steps, feet under body
  • Common beginner figures: forward two-step, box, two turning two-steps

Waltz Essentials:

  • Rhythm: 1-2-3, 1-2-3 (3/4 time)
  • Rise and fall technique (rise on steps 2-3, fall on 1)
  • Box step is foundation
  • Flows and progresses around the floor

Optional Paid Resources (Roundalab)

If you want even more comprehensive materials:

  • Roundalab.org has subscription-based video figure libraries
  • Teaching DVDs for each phase
  • These are comprehensive but not necessary if you're using the free resources

Tips for Success

  1. Start with RDInstruction.co.uk - Work through both courses systematically
  2. Practice together regularly - Even 20 minutes helps
  3. Focus on basics first - Solid fundamentals matter more than fancy figures
  4. Use a speaker - Practice with waltz and two-step music playing from your playlists
  5. Don't stress about perfection - The cuer will guide you during class

How to Use Your Playlists

Week 1-2:

  • Start with the slower playlists exclusively
  • Practice 20-30 minutes, 3-4 times per week
  • Focus on one rhythm per practice session

Week 2-3:

  • Mix in songs from the moderate playlists
  • Alternate: 2 slow songs, then 1 moderate song
  • Increase practice to 30-40 minutes as you build stamina

Pro Tips:

  • Start each practice with your slowest songs to warm up
  • The key is consistent practice at the right tempo
  • These songs will help you rebuild muscle memory without overwhelming you
  • By week 3, you'll be ready to jump back into those lessons!

When You're Ready to Jump Back In

After 2-3 weeks of focused practice, you should be comfortable with Phase I and early Phase II figures in both rhythms. This will let you jump back into classes without feeling lost. The cuer will introduce new figures gradually, and you'll remember more than you think once you're back in the flow!

Remember: The most important thing is to have fun! Round dancing is about enjoying the music, the movement, and the time with Brianna. Don't worry about being perfect - just get out there and dance!
